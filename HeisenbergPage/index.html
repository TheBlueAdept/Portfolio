Introduction to VQE and the Heisenberg XYZ Model

The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) is a hybrid quantum-classical algorithm designed to determine the ground state energy of complex quantum systems. Utilizing a parametrized quantum circuit (ansatz), it evaluates energy expectations on a quantum simulator or processor while classical routines iteratively optimize the parameters to minimize the energy. This approach efficiently leverages quantum mechanics for computationally intensive problems.

The Heisenberg XYZ model generalizes the Ising model by considering quantum spin interactions in three spatial directions (X, Y, Z) with distinct coupling constants. It represents a 1D lattice of spin-½ particles with nearest-neighbor interactions, allowing anisotropic coupling along different axes. This model provides deep insight into magnetic systems and quantum phase transitions. Being a quantum system, it is best modelled by VQE to find the ground state energy of the system.

Simulation Details

This simulation uses a 5-qubit Heisenberg XYZ model with periodic boundary conditions and no external magnetic field. Three ansatz circuits—TwoLocal, EfficientSU2, and UCCSD—are tested with Conjugate Gradient and Powell optimization methods. Executed on Qiskit’s AerSimulator due to limited access to real quantum devices, the study compares accuracy, time, and iterations.
